##  Project Overview

This project analyzes anime data from the **MyAnimeList 2023 dataset**, exploring how factors like **genre**, **source material**, **type**, and **studio** influence anime popularity and ratings.

Using **Python**, **SQL Server**, and **Tableau**, I cleaned, queried, and visualized the dataset to uncover insights into the anime industry ‚Äî from which studios produce the highest-rated shows to how different genres perform on average.

##  Key Insights

###  Popularity vs. Score  
A scatter plot comparing **Members (popularity)** and **Ratings** shows that while some anime like *Naruto* and *Bleach* have huge fanbases, their ratings are not always the highest ‚Äî suggesting that **popularity doesn‚Äôt always equal quality**.

---

###  Average Ratings by Genre  
Genres like **Action**, **Drama**, and **Adventure** dominate in volume and tend to score consistently above average.  
Lighter genres such as **Comedy** and **Romance** have more variability in ratings.

---

###  Anime Count by Type  
TV series overwhelmingly dominate the anime landscape, followed by **OVAs**, **Movies**, and **Specials** ‚Äî reflecting how serialized content remains the most common form.

---

###  Source Material vs. Rating  
Anime adapted from **Manga** and **Original stories** receive the highest average ratings, while **Game-based adaptations** tend to score lower, hinting that strong narrative foundations matter.

---

### Top Studios by Average Score  
Studios like **Bones**, **Sunrise**, and **Toei Animation** consistently achieve high average scores, establishing their reputation for quality animation.

---

###  Top 10 Most Popular Anime  
Fan-favorites such as *Attack on Titan*, *Death Note*, and *Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood* lead the charts ‚Äî showing enduring popularity across years.

##  Methodology

1. **Data Cleaning (Python):**  
   - Removed duplicates and missing values  
   - Standardized genres and text formatting  
   - Converted numeric columns to proper data types  

2. **SQL Analysis (SSMS):**  
   - Created views for aggregation  
   - Queried top genres, studios, and source materials  
   - Prepared summarized tables for Tableau  

3. **Tableau Visualization:**  
   - Created multiple charts and dashboards  
   - Built an interactive story for visual storytelling  

---

##  Results & Storytelling

The **Anime Data Analytics Dashboard** highlights that:
- Anime popularity is driven more by community and legacy than just high ratings.
- Manga and original concepts often produce better-rated shows.
- TV format dominates production volume.
- A few powerhouse studios drive much of the high-rated content.
